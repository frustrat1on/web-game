History of the Michelin Guide

The Michelin Guide began in 1900 as a free road guide. It became a paid publication in 1922 and started restaurant evaluations in 1926. Over the years, it gained a reputation for strict standards, earning the title of the "gourmet Bible."

What is Michelin Star?

A Michelin Star is awarded annually to restaurants with outstanding cooking. Five key criteria are used: ingredient quality, flavor harmony, technique mastery, the chef's personality in the food.
